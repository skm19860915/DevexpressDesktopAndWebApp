Description

This adults-only, all-inclusive resort is set beachfront in the lively Cancun hotel zone. Accommodations are comprised of 371 contemporary rooms and suites. Guests of the Aqua Club will also enjoy access to an exclusive lounge serving daily continental breakfast, hors d'oeuvres and top-shelf spirits. The resort features seven restaurants, and a selection of posh bars and lounges. In addition to an oceanfront infinity pool, guests will also enjoy a collection of smaller pools with varying water temperatures, a swim-up bar and poolside concierge service. The boutique spa offers a hydrotherapy circuit and a comprehensive selection of rejuvenating treatments. Complimentary wireless internet access is offered throughout the resort. At your arrival enjoy a relaxing hand massage and relax yourself with the different aromas in the resort.

I booked an all inclusive package with live aqua for my honeymoon/ birthday. I was informed they are not decorating the rooms for any occasions because of covid restrictions and was immediately in touch with Sophie. Upon arrival, Sophie (Vip manager), one of the head bartenders Pablo, and Jorge (in charge of operations) escorted us to our room with a surprise of champagne and cake. The rooms are beautiful and the sheets are new or either extremely taken care of. Their service is 10 stars. I cannot even explain how hard these people try to accommodate to our service. All the workers at live aqua (BESIDES ONE, which I will get into later) are always checking up on you. They greet you with a genuine smile. The top chefs come around asking for your input regarding last nights dinner and if there’s anything they can improve. Their goal is to make sure the guests are satisfied in every way. I do have to say, this is definitely a very quiet, more chill, and older couple resort. The only entertainment they have is either someone playing the saxophone or singing. I didn’t mind this because I just wanted to relax and not be around some wild kids anyways. But just a heads up for others. The ONLY complaint I have is the cabanas and the female worker who works at the reservation cabana site. If you don’t go reserve a beach chair or a first come first serve cabana, then good luck. You have to literally grab a spot at 6/7am , leave a green towel there, and come back whenever. Usually all the beach chairs are taken by 8am. Most of the cabanas are booked and you have to reserve a day before. The female worker didn’t explain this properly. She even gave an attitude a couple times when my husband asked her and a worker next to her to cover one of the cabana beds that we reserved. She said “there’s other people waiting”, yet we were the FIRST ones there and the FIRST ones to ask for someone to set our cabana up. Also, if you see a cabana with a sign on the bed that says “are you interested in this cabana ? Ask a worker” (something in between those lines), this sign means the cabana is reserved. I don’t understand how the hell this means it’s reserved. They need to make a sign that says RESERVED and put it on the cabana if that’s the case. This whole cabana reserving and first come first serve at 7am was just too stressful. They need a better system for this. The names that I can definitely remember and that delivered amazing service were Pablo (head bartender), Daniel (Working at the cabanas), Marco (juice bar), Omar (siete Restaurant).
